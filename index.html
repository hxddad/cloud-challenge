ENG 1101 - Ethics, Communication & Problem Solving

This will be your first ever ENG course, which will cover engineering ethics and case studies, communication and problem solving. Straightforward course, but quite time-consuming.

Difficulty: 3/10 (Easy).

EECS 1011 - Computational Thinking Through Mechatronics (MATLAB)

This will be your very first programming class in your undergraduate studies, with a C-like programming language used by scientists and engineers called MATLAB (short for Matrix Laboratory). This course also assumes you never programmed before...

Difficulty: 2/10 if known at least one programming language, otherwise 4/10 (Easy).

MATH 1013 - Applied Calculus 1

Behold your very first calculus course at university, which touches on differential calculus. It's basically review from high school calculus...

Difficulty: 5/10 (Medium).

MATH 1025 - Applied Linear Algebra

You will learn about and apply linear algebra concepts such as matrices, vectors, linear transformations...

Difficulty: 5/10 (Medium).

PHYS 1800 - Engineering Mechanics

This first physics course you will be taking may mostly be review of high school physics...

Difficulty: 6/10 (Medium).

ENG 1102 - Engineering Design Principles

This ENG course will be your first design course...

Difficulty: 3/10 (Easy).

CHEM 1100 - Chemistry & Materials Science

This is your first (and probably last) chemistry course in the program...

Difficulty: 6/10 (Medium).

EECS 1021 - OOP from Sensors to Actuators (Java)

This is the same premise as EECS 1011, but you will be learning and using the Java programming language...

Difficulty: 4/10 (Medium).

MATH 1014 - Applied Calculus 2

This is the hardest, but the most interesting calculus course in my opinion...

Difficulty: 7/10 (Hard).

PHYS 1801 - Electricity, Magnetism and Optics

Just as the course title says, you will be learning about E&M and optics...

Difficulty: 6/10 (Medium).

EECS 1028 - Discrete Mathematics for Engineers

Let me introduce you to the most infamous course to every first year EECS engineering student...

Difficulty: 8/10 (Hard).
